Câu 1:
In her last job, Gayle knew how everything worked. Her organization had formal and informal standards in place regarding how business analysis should be performed. But after leaving her last job, she just landed a Sr. Business Analyst job with ABC company and to her surprise there are no defined standards in place. Gayle determines that she needs to define a requirements management life cycle approach for the initiative she has been assigned to. Which requirements lifecycle processes should Gayle define?

A. Gayle should define processes for stakeholder engagement, business analysis governance, and information management.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 These are all tasks in Business Analysis Planning and Monitoring. BABOK 3.0

B. Gayle should define processes for tracing, prioritizing, assessing changes to, maintaining, and approving requirements.
   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 Requirements Life Cycle Management includes tracing requirements, maintaining requirements, prioritizing requirements, assessing requirements changes, and approving requirements. BABOK 5.0

C. Gayle should define processes for preparing for elicitation, conducting elicitation, and managing stakeholder collaboration.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 These are all tasks in Elicitation and Collaboration. BABOK 4.0

D. Gayle should define processes for analyzing the current state and future state, assessing risks and defining a change strategy.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 These are all tasks in Strategy Analysis. BABOK 6.0

➤ ĐÁP ÁN ĐÚNG: Gayle should define processes for tracing, prioritizing, assessing changes to, maintaining, and approving requirements.

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
Requirements Life Cycle Management includes tracing requirements, maintaining requirements, prioritizing requirements, assessing requirements changes, and approving requirements. BABOK 5.0

Câu 4:
Suppose you discover that stakeholders are complaining about the requirements for a product. You found that some of the requirements in the review session only applied to a division in Brazil, and did not pertain to those in the US and Europe. You realize that in presenting the requirements you should have:

A. Held separate interviews with each stakeholder group in order to determine their unique requirements.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Separate interviews may or may not have been appropriate. Nothing in the BABOK says that you need to have separate interviews and is an example of being too prescriptive. Watch for those kinds of answers on the actual exam.

B. Told the Brazilian division that their requirements were out-of-scope.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 They might have been out-of-scope, and if so, should have been dealt with earlier in the requirements life cycle, such as when assessing requirements changes. Besides, this is not the responsibility of a business analyst, but part of the change control approach in effect. BABOK 5.4.2 and 5.5.2

C. Determined which requirements were relevant to which stakeholder groups and presented those in an appropriate format.
   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 The task of a business analyst is to ensure the solution requirements and designs are aligned to one another. BABOK Chapter 5 Introduction and 4.4.2

D. Determined the appropriate stakeholder groups. The Brazilian division had such unique requirements, that their requirements should not have been taken into account.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Determining that a group's requirements should not be taken into account is not the responsibility of the business analyst. BABOK 5.4.2 and 5.5.2

➤ ĐÁP ÁN ĐÚNG: Determined which requirements were relevant to which stakeholder groups and presented those in an appropriate format.

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
