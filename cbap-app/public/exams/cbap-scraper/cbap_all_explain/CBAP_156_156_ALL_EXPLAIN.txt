   ❌ SAI

   ✅ ĐÚNG

Câu 3:
Generally, when would a business analyst model requirements in a change initiative?

A. Before requirements elicitation.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 The business analyst must elicit at least some information before they can model the requirements, otherwise what would they model? BABOK 7.1.1 and 7.1.3

B. After evaluating the solution.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Solution evaluation assesses the performance of and value delivered by a solution or solution component in use by the enterprise. At this point modeling would have generally been completed before implementation of the solution or solution component. BABOK 8.0

C. After requirements elicitation.
   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 Requirements elicitation generally precedes analysis and is stated as an input into specify and model requirements task. Although the BABOK states elicitation and modelling may occur sequentially, iteratively, or concurrently, you must start with some amount of elicited information before you can model that elicited information. BABOK 7.1.3

D. At any point in a change initiative.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 While the business analyst may model at any time during a change initiative, they generally have to have elicited some information before they can attempt to model that information. BABOK 7.1.1 and 7.1.3

➤ ĐÁP ÁN ĐÚNG: After requirements elicitation.

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
Requirements elicitation generally precedes analysis and is stated as an input into specify and model requirements task. Although the BABOK states elicitation and modelling may occur sequentially, iteratively, or concurrently, you must start with some amount of elicited information before you can model that elicited information. BABOK 7.1.3

Câu 4:
A junior business analyst is modeling requirements in order to identify and validate inputs and outputs of the solution she is modeling. She approaches James, the senior business analyst, for advice. What modeling technique would James advise the junior business analyst to use?

A. Interface analysis
   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 This is the technique used in the specify and model requirements task that James would recommend. BABOK 7.1.6 and 10.24

B. Prototyping
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Since the business analyst is validating inputs and outputs only, prototyping is not the best technique. Prototyping is used to identify missing or improper requirements. BABOK 7.1.6 and 10.36

   ❌ SAI
   📝 Process modeling is used to show the steps or activities that are performed in the organization, but not used to validate inputs and outputs of the solution. BABOK 7.1.6 and 10.35

   ❌ SAI
   📝 This is not a modeling technique. It is a technique used mostly with Agile approaches for understanding which features will help drive customer satisfaction. BABOK Table 11.1.2

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
This is the technique used in the specify and model requirements task that James would recommend. BABOK 7.1.6 and 10.24
