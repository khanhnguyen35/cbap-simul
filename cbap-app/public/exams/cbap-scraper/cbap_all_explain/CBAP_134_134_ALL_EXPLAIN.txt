Câu 1:
Monique is analyzing historical data to identify information relevant to her current project. Which type of elicitation is Monique utilizing?

A. Research
   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 Research involves systematically discovering and studying information from materials or sources that are not directly known by stakeholders to identify trends or past results. Stakeholders can still participate in the research. BABOK 4.2.2

B. Experiments
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Experiments involve identifying information that could not be known without some sort of controlled test. Experiments involve observational studies, proofs of concept, and prototypes. The scenario is describing research. BABOK 4.2.2

C. Document analysis
   ❌ SAI
   📝 This is a close second! However, document analysis examines materials that describe either the business environment or existing organizational assets vs. historical data. In addition, document analysis is a technique vs. an elicitation type (experiments, collaborative, or research). The scenario is describing research. BABOK 10.18.1 and 4.2.2

D. Collaborative
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Collaborative involves direct interaction with stakeholders and relies on stakeholders' experiences, expertise, and judgment. The scenario is describing research. BABOK 4.2.2

➤ ĐÁP ÁN ĐÚNG: Research

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
Research involves systematically discovering and studying information from materials or sources that are not directly known by stakeholders to identify trends or past results. Stakeholders can still participate in the research. BABOK 4.2.2

Câu 11:
Shelby is eliciting requirements for a new program intended to increase employee satisfaction. Recent surveys indicated that employees did not feel recognized for a job well done, but the sponsor is not sure a "Gold Star Award" would bolster employee satisfaction results at their company. What could Shelby suggest?

A. Suggest the "Gold Star Award" be piloted and then assess the results before moving forward.
   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 This is a type of elicitation called "experiments" and is used when information is unknown without some sort of controlled test. BABOK 4.2.2

B. Suggest that a quick survey be distributed to see what employees think of the idea.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 This would be a close answer, but a survey will not provide detailed requirements and may work against the overall objective if the program is queried but not implemented. In addition, response rates on surveys are often too low for a thorough understanding of a topic and any ambiguous questions may be left unanswered or answered incorrectly. Given this is a new award program which people know little about, it may be difficult to get employee's input on something that doesn't exist. In this case it might be better to conduct a pilot of the award program to get more realistic feedback. BABOK 4.2.2

C. Researching other companies to see what they do and then benchmark against existing programs.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 The scenario suggests that the sponsor isn't sure that the "Gold Star Award" would work at their company. Researching and benchmarking will not change that. The scenario is calling for a pilot which is an "experiment" elicitation type. BABOK 4.2.2

D. Scrap the idea because it is too risky.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 This is a little hasty; the idea had to be elicited from a company source. The scenario is calling for a pilot which is an "experiment" elicitation type. BABOK 4.2.2

➤ ĐÁP ÁN ĐÚNG: Suggest the "Gold Star Award" be piloted and then assess the results before moving forward.

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
This is a type of elicitation called "experiments" and is used when information is unknown without some sort of controlled test. BABOK 4.2.2
