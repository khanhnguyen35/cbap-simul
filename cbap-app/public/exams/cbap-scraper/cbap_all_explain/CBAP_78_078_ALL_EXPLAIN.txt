Câu 1:
Rhonda is a BA reviewing the issues that surfaced on a similar project to the one she is currently assigned to that was closed two years ago. What would be the value in reviewing past issues from similar projects?

A. Reviewing past issues and their fixes could help establish the amount of money needed for the contingency reserves.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 While reviewing the financial information may help understand the quantitative impacts of the issues, it is not the primary purpose.

B. Reviewing past issues could help identify risks for the current project.
   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 Manifested risks are issues. Typically, using the Lessons Learned technique to proactively manage risks to prevent issues costs less and delivers more value. BABOK 6.3.6

C. Reviewing past issues could help identify the fix so the team won't have to recreate the wheel.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 While true, it would be more helpful to prevent the issue from reoccurring.

D. Reviewing past issues will help Rhonda understand which stakeholder groups were impacted and so will assist in stakeholder identification.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 While stakeholder identification may be a side benefit, it is not the primary purpose.

➤ ĐÁP ÁN ĐÚNG: Reviewing past issues could help identify risks for the current project.

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
Manifested risks are issues. Typically, using the Lessons Learned technique to proactively manage risks to prevent issues costs less and delivers more value. BABOK 6.3.6

Câu 4:
Sofia is a BA who has just helped a business owner define a business need relating to a new service that her company wants to offer. The company is expecting this new service to increase revenues, increase market share, and reduce customer complaints. She, along with key stakeholders, have generated alternative change strategies related to the new service and are completing business cases to support a recommendation. Sofia realizes that she has no idea of the potential business and technical ramifications or magnitude of the effort. She realizes she needs to step back and complete which task/element/technique next?

A. Sofia and her team need to identify risks.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 This is true but is just the first step and so is not comprehensive enough. BABOK 6.3.1

B. Sofia and her team need to analyze constraints, assumptions, and dependencies associated with their recommended solution.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 This is true but all risks need to be analyzed. Analyzing these three things will not address all risks. BABOK 6.3.1

C. Sofia and her team need to assess risks.
   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 Risks speak to uncertainty and "potential" impacts. BABOK 6.3.1

D. Sofia and her team need to define the potential negative impact to value of the recommended solution.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 This is too specific and is just one component of qualitatively assessing risks. BABOK 6.3.1

➤ ĐÁP ÁN ĐÚNG: Sofia and her team need to assess risks.

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
Risks speak to uncertainty and "potential" impacts. BABOK 6.3.1
