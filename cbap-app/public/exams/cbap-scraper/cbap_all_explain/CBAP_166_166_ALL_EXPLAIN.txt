Câu 4:
The purpose of tracing requirements is best described by which of the following statements:

A. Traceability assists in managing accountability for requirements activities that are performed by stakeholders outside of the requirements team.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Traceability refers to the requirements, not to who does it. BABOK 5.1.1

B. Ensuring that requirements and designs at different levels are aligned to one another, and to manage the effects of change to one level on related requirements."
   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 Purpose as listed in the BABOK 5.1.1

C. Traceability metrics are used by the Change Management Board to approve or deny requests for change.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 There is no such thing as traceability metrics, there may or may not be a Change Management board, and traceability is not used to approve or deny change requests. BABOK 5.1.1

D. Traceability requirements supplement business requirements and functional requirements.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Invented answer. There is no such thing as a traceability requirement. Requirements must be traceable to be considered 'good,' but there is no category of requirement called 'traceability.' BABOK 2.3 and 5.1.1

➤ ĐÁP ÁN ĐÚNG: Ensuring that requirements and designs at different levels are aligned to one another, and to manage the effects of change to one level on related requirements."

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
Purpose as listed in the BABOK 5.1.1

   ❌ SAI

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
