Câu 3:
Gracie owns a small office building. She recently changed cleaning services to reduce expenses. During the first week of the new service, she discovered that the change resulted in a change in the frequency of cleaning the bathrooms, as well as a change in the way tenants handled their trash.    📝 A business case would not have helped Gracie given the undesirable outcomes.

   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 This is another way of saying "acceptance and evaluation criteria" and "vendor assessment"; both of which would've been valuable techniques. BABOK 6.2.6

Câu 7:
Sean, a BA, is working with a business owner to clarify the organization's business objectives. The business owner keeps talking about the current situation and a new project to automate a cumbersome business process. Sean tries to bring her back to the business objectives. Finally the business owner says that she has three business objectives: increase employee satisfaction, improve revenues by increasing sales, and comply with new regulations. Sean is not comfortable with these business objectives. Why?

A. They are stated but not confirmed.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Objectives do not include confirmation. BABOK 6.2.4.1

B. They do not state the business problem or opportunity.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Objectives do not state the business problem or opportunity, they describe what the organization is seeking to achieve. BABOK 6.2.4.1

C. They are not measurable.
   ✅ ĐÚNG
   📝 Objectives need to be SMART: specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bounded. Also, the stated objectives are lacking in the "specific" and "time-bounded" aspects. BABOK 6.2.4.1

D. They do not state the business benefits.
   ❌ SAI
   📝 Objectives do not include business benefits. In addition, objectives need to be SMART. BABOK 6.2.4.1

➤ ĐÁP ÁN ĐÚNG: They are not measurable.

🔥 GIẢI THÍCH CHI TIẾT (BABOK):
Objectives need to be SMART: specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bounded. Also, the stated objectives are lacking in the "specific" and "time-bounded" aspects. BABOK 6.2.4.1
